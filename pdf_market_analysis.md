However, there are **specific, high-value PDF problems** that the giants ignore or charge for. These are your opportunities.

### 1. "PDF to Excel" (Finance Niche)
*   **Why**: Accountants and data analysts *hate* manually typing data from PDF bank statements into Excel.
*   **The Pain**: Most free converters scrape the text but ruin the table structure.
*   **The Opportunity**: If your tool keeps the rows/columns perfect, you can charge $5/mo or show high-RPM finance ads.
*   **Spin-off Idea**: `TableExtractor.io` or `BankStatementConverter.com`.

### 2. "PDF Filler / Signer" (Legal/Real Estate Niche)
*   **Why**: People need to sign a lease or contract *now* on their phone.
*   **The Pain**: Adobe is expensive ($20/mo). DocuSign is complex.
*   **The Opportunity**: A simple, mobile-friendly "Upload -> Sign -> Download" tool.
*   **Spin-off Idea**: `QuickSignPDF.com`.

### 3. "Invoice to PDF Generator" (Freelance Niche)
*   **Why**: New freelancers need to look professional.
*   **The Opportunity**: This isn't just a converter; it's a *generator*. You capture the user at the start of their business journey (high ad value).

## Summary Strategy for PDF Tools

1.  **Keep the Basics Here**: Maintain the standard suite (Merge, Split, Compress) on ToolHub to keep users happy and on the site longer.
2.  **Don't Fight the Giants**: Do not try to be the next "iLovePDF". You will lose.
3.  **Hunt the Niches**: Focus on **Financial** (PDF to Excel) or **Legal** (Sign/Fill) problems if you want to build a separate PDF business.
